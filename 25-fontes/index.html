Book it. PR6 on SIMplifica

The 25 Fontes is one of Madeira's most popular walks, so slots go fast. In order of what matters:

  1. Book your €4.50 slot on SIMplifica in advance. Entry is in 30-minute windows; in summer the trail sells out days ahead. Under-12s are free but must still be on the booking.
  2. Aim for early or late slots. The lagoon and the narrow levada get crowded midday; first or last entries are calmer and cooler.
  3. Or go with an IFCN protocol operator — a lower trail fee (€3) plus transport from Funchal, which solves the long mountain drive.
PR6 is paid via SIMplifica and not included in the multi-day passes on their own separate booking. €4.50 per person; under-12s and residents free but counted on the reservation. Check the live status above before you pay.

Getting there. Rabaçal & the road down

The trail starts at Rabaçal, high on the west of the island — a long, winding mountain drive from Funchal (about an hour and a half).

  • Park at the Rabaçal car park on the mountain road (ER110/ER105). You cannot drive down to the trailhead.
  • From the car park it's about a 2 km walk — roughly 300 m of descent — down the narrow tarmac access road to the Rabaçal forestry houses, where the levada begins. A small paid shuttle van also runs it if you'd rather not walk down.
  • That descent is the sting in the tail: it's easy going down, but you climb it back up at the end, tired. Factor it into your timing.
There's no fuel, shop or reliable signal up at Rabaçal. Bring water, layers and anything you'll need for the day before you leave the coast.

Make a day of it

Two short add-ons share the same start, so most people combine them:

  • Levada do Risco (PR6.1) — a 20-minute stroll to a tall, misty waterfall. Its own €4.50 booking.
  • Vereda da Lagoa do Vento (PR6.3) — a steeper extension to a hidden lagoon and waterfall for the keen.

Closed or full? Your options

If IFCN closes PR6 (weather, landslip, works) or you can't get a slot:

  • Reschedule via the SIMplifica call centre before your date — you can move date/time freely, and switch trail only if yours was officially closed.
  • Open alternatives: check the live board — Risco (PR6.1), Caldeirão Verde (PR9) and Balcões (PR11) are usually available and just as green.

Three things people find out too late

  • The return is uphill. The 2 km access road you skipped down at the start is a real climb at the end — pace yourself and keep water.
  • It's laurel forest, not a beach day. Rabaçal sits at ~1,000–1,300 m; it can be cool, damp or foggy while Funchal is sunny. Bring a layer and grippy shoes — the levada edge is narrow with drops.
  • It's out-and-back. You return along the same levada, so turn around with enough time and daylight for the climb out.
