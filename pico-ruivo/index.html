Book it. PR1.2 on SIMplifica

This is the short route to the summit, and a sunrise favourite, so slots go fast.

  1. Book your €4.50 slot on SIMplifica in advance, in a 30-minute window. Under-12s are free but must be on the booking.
  2. Sunrise slots go first. If you want the summit at dawn, book early and expect company; mid-morning is quieter.
  3. Coming off PR1? If you cross from Pico do Areeiro on PR1, you finish above here — see getting back from PR1.
PR1.2 is €4.50 per person, paid on SIMplifica; under-12s and residents free but counted on the reservation. Check the live status above before you drive up.

Getting there. Achada do Teixeira

The walk starts at the Achada do Teixeira car park, high above Santana on the north side — about an hour's drive from Funchal.

  • Drive to the Achada do Teixeira car park (this is also where PR1 finishes). There's a shelter and a viewpoint; parking is limited at sunrise.
  • From the car park, a built stone path climbs steadily to the Pico Ruivo mountain shelter (Casa de Abrigo), then a short final push to the 1,862 m summit — Madeira's highest point.
  • It's the easiest way to the top: no exposed ridge, just a steady paved climb of roughly 200 m of ascent.

Closed or full? Your options

If IFCN closes PR1.2 (weather, works) or you can't get a slot:

  • Reschedule via the SIMplifica call centre before your date — date/time freely, another trail only if yours was officially closed.
  • Open alternatives: check the live board — Caldeirão Verde (PR9) and Balcões (PR11) are usually available.

Three things people find out too late

  • Summit weather ≠ Funchal weather. At 1,862 m it can be cold, windy and in cloud while the coast is sunny — you may be above a sea of clouds, or in one. Bring a warm layer and check the live status.
  • It's a climb, not a stroll. The stone path gains height steadily to the shelter; easy underfoot but a proper uphill, especially at altitude.
  • Nothing's open up there. The shelter is basic — no shop or reliable signal. Carry water, snacks and a headtorch for a dark start.
