Book it. PR14 on SIMplifica

The Levada dos Cedros is one of the quieter laurel-forest walks — a gentle descent, not a big-name honeypot — but it's still a paid, booking-only PR trail:

  1. Book your €4.50 slot on SIMplifica in advance. Under-12s and residents are free but must still be on the booking. Check the live status above before you pay.
  2. Sort your return first. This is a point-to-point walk (see below) — decide how you'll get back before you book the day.
  3. Or go with an IFCN protocol operator — a lower trail fee (€3) plus transport, which handles both the long drive west and the one-way logistics.
PR14 is paid via SIMplifica on its own separate booking and not included in the multi-day passes. €4.50 per person; under-12s and residents free but counted on the reservation.

Getting there. Fanal & the Paul da Serra

The walk starts at Fanal, high on the Paul da Serra plateau in the west — the same place as the famous ancient til trees. It's a long drive from Funchal (about 1h15–1h30) across open moorland.

  • Park at Fanal, where the levada path drops into the forest. The plateau road (ER209) is exposed and often deep in cloud — drive slowly and don't rely on the view.
  • It's point-to-point and downhill, finishing near Ribeira da Janela where it meets the PR15. You won't come back to your car on foot without a long climb.
  • Getting back: two cars, a pre-booked taxi/pickup at the bottom, a guided walk with transfers, or continue on the PR15 down to Ribeira da Janela village for the coast road.
No shop, fuel or reliable signal on the Paul da Serra. Bring water, warm and waterproof layers, and a torch — Fanal is cold and wet far more often than the coast, even in summer.

What it's like

This is deep-forest walking at its best: an almost-level, then gently descending levada through the laurisilva — the UNESCO World Heritage laurel forest — dripping with moss and lichen, ferns crowding the path, birdsong the only sound.

  • Atmospheric and often misty — the cloud that frustrates viewpoint-hunters makes this forest magical.
  • Pairs naturally with Fanal (PR13), which starts from the same spot — do the short Fanal loop for the famous gnarled trees, then walk the Cedros down.
  • Quiet. Far fewer people than Rabaçal; if you want the forest to yourself, this is it.

Closed or full? Your options

If IFCN closes PR14 (weather, fallen trees, works) or you can't get a slot:

  • Reschedule via the SIMplifica call centre before your date — you can move date/time freely, and switch trail only if yours was officially closed.
  • Open alternatives nearby: check the live board — Fanal (PR13) is right there, and 25 Fontes (PR6) at Rabaçal is a short drive south.

Three things people find out too late

  • It doesn't loop back. You finish near Ribeira da Janela, not at Fanal — fix the return before you set off, or you're facing a long uphill road walk.
  • Fanal is cloud country. It's often cold, wet and white-out foggy on the plateau while Funchal is sunny — pack for rain and bring a torch for the dim forest and any tunnels.
  • The path is slick. Moss, mud and roots make the descent slippery; grippy shoes and poles help, especially after rain.
