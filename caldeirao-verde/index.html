Book it. PR9 on SIMplifica

A long, green classic through tunnels to a 100-metre waterfall — and a busy one.

  1. Book your €4.50 slot on SIMplifica in advance, in a 30-minute window. Under-12s free but on the booking.
  2. Start early. It's a 5–6 hour day; an early slot gives you daylight, cooler tunnels and thinner crowds.
  3. Parking at Queimadas is about €2.00/hour — bring change and factor it in for a full-day walk.
PR9 is €4.50 per person on SIMplifica, plus ~€2.00/hour parking at Queimadas. Under-12s and residents free but counted. Check the live status above before you drive north.

Getting there. Queimadas (Santana)

The walk starts at the Queimadas Forest Park, above Santana on the green north coast — about an hour from Funchal.

  • Drive to the Queimadas car park and its pretty thatched houses; parking is paid (~€2.00/hour). The levada path begins here.
  • It's a long, mostly level walk along an 18th-century levada through Laurissilva forest to the Caldeirão Verde — a green pool under a ~100 m cascade.
  • Keen walkers continue on the full PR9 to Caldeirão do Inferno (about 8.7 km each way) — much longer; most turn around at the Caldeirão Verde waterfall.

Bring a torch. The tunnels & the drops

  • Four tunnels are carved into the rock — some long, dark and dripping. A headtorch (or phone torch) is essential; it's genuinely dark inside.
  • The levada edge is narrow with big drops in places, often with a handrail — not one for a strong fear of heights.
  • Wear grippy shoes and expect wet feet; the north side is damp and the tunnels drip.

Closed or full? Your options

North-side trails close for weather and landslips. If PR9 is closed or full:

  • Reschedule via the SIMplifica call centre before your date.
  • Open alternatives: check the live board — 25 Fontes (PR6) and Balcões (PR11) are usually available and just as green.

Three things people find out too late

  • It's a full day. 5–6 hours and ~13 km there and back — start early and carry water and food.
  • The tunnels are dark and wet. No torch means feeling your way in the dark; bring one and grippy shoes.
  • North and damp. It can be foggy, wet and cooler than the coast, and the ledges are narrow — go steady and mind the drops.
